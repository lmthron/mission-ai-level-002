Generating Excel Formulas

How to Describe Your Need

A good description includes four elements:

  • Column layout: What's in columns A, B, C, etc.
  • What to calculate: Sum, average, count, etc.
  • Conditions: What criteria to apply
  • Where the result goes: Which cell or range

‚ùå Vague Request

"I need a formula to calculate revenue"

‚úì Clear Request

"I have customer data in columns A-E. Column B is Tier, Column C is Revenue, Column E is Status. I need total revenue from Active Enterprise customers in cell F2."

Common Formula Categories

Conditional Calculations

SUMIF, AVERAGEIF, COUNTIF (single condition) or SUMIFS, AVERAGEIFS, COUNTIFS (multiple conditions)

Lookups

VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP - Finding data in other tables; INDEX/MATCH - More flexible lookups

Text Manipulation

CONCATENATE/CONCAT - Combining text; LEFT, RIGHT, MID - Extracting parts; TRIM, CLEAN - Removing spaces/characters

Date Calculations

DATEDIF - Time between dates; EOMONTH - End of month; NETWORKDAYS - Business days

Statistical

MEDIAN, MODE - Central tendency; STDEV, VAR - Variability; PERCENTILE, QUARTILE - Distribution

Key Point:

You don't need to memorize formula names. Just describe what you want to calculate and Claude will give you the right formula.

Data Cleaning and Transformation

Common Data Problems

  • Inconsistent formatting: Dates in different formats, mixed case text, numbers stored as text
  • Missing or incomplete data: Blank cells, placeholder text ("N/A", "TBD")
  • Duplicate entries: Same records with slight variations

Data Transformation Workflow

Step 1
Upload or Describe Data
Upload small files or describe structure for larger datasets
Step 2
Explain What's Wrong
Describe the inconsistency or problem clearly
Step 3
Get Claude's Solution
Claude provides formula, instructions, where to put it, how to copy down
Step 4
Test on a Few Rows
Don't apply to entire dataset immediately. Test on 5-10 rows first.
Step 5
Verify and Apply to Full Dataset
After confirming it works, apply to all your data

Detecting Patterns and Trends

What Claude Looks For

  • Increases and decreases over time
  • Outliers (unusually high or low values)
  • Clusters (groups of similar values)
  • Correlations (when two things move together)
  • Seasonality (recurring patterns by time period)

How it works: Claude reads through your data like a human analyst would and describes what stands out based on its training.

How to Ask for Pattern Analysis

Upload your data or describe it, then ask:

  • "What trends do you see in this sales data?"
  • "Are there any outliers or unusual patterns?"
  • "What correlations exist between these variables?"
  • "Do you see any seasonal patterns?"
Creating Visualizations

What Claude Does (and Doesn't Do)

Claude DOES NOT: Create the actual charts in Excel (it can't directly manipulate your Excel file)

Claude DOES guide you on:

  • Which chart type to use for your specific data and goals
  • How to set up the chart
  • What data to include
  • Best practices for clarity and impact

How to Ask for Chart Guidance

Describe your data structure and your goal:

"I have quarterly revenue data by product category (4 quarters, 5 categories). What chart type should I use to show trends over time?"

Claude will recommend the chart type and explain why it's the best choice for your needs.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Pitfall 1: Vague Descriptions

Problem: "I need a formula for sales"

Solution: Include column layout, what to calculate, conditions, and where result goes

Pitfall 2: Not Testing Formulas

Problem: Applying formulas to entire dataset without testing

Solution: Always test on 5-10 rows first, verify results, then apply broadly

Pitfall 3: Blindly Trusting Results

Problem: Not verifying Claude's calculations

Solution: Spot-check formulas manually, verify totals make sense, ask Claude to explain surprising findings

Pitfall 4: Overcomplicating Simple Tasks

Problem: Using AI for analysis that takes 2 minutes to do manually

Solution: Ask yourself: Is this repetitive? Will explaining to Claude take longer? Is this high value?
