Advanced Prompting Techniques

üß† Chain-of-Thought

Asking Claude to show its reasoning step-by-step before giving the final answer.

Why it works: When Claude explains its thinking, it catches logical errors and produces more accurate results.

Use for: Complex decisions, technical problem-solving, strategic planning Avoid for: Routine tasks where reasoning doesn't add value

üìã Few-Shot Examples

Showing Claude 2-3 examples of what you want before asking for the real output.

Why it works: Examples are clearer than descriptions. Claude learns your exact format and style.

Use for: Complex formatting, specific style/structure, formats you'll reuse Avoid for: One-off tasks, simple requests

Chain-of-Thought Template

Simple trigger phrase: Add "Think through this step-by-step:" to your prompt.

Example: "We need to decide between Feature A and Feature B. Think through this step-by-step: 1. What are the business impacts? 2. What are the technical complexities? 3. What are the resource requirements? Then recommend which to prioritize."

Strategic Use:

Chain-of-thought and few-shot take more time and tokens. Use them when quality matters most, not for every task. Save chain-of-thought for important decisions. Save few-shot prompts as Skills for formats you'll reuse repeatedly.

Connecting to Your Tools

What Are Connectors?

Connectors allow Claude to access information from your systems directly. Instead of downloading and uploading documents, Claude can search your tools, read pages, and reference current information.

Examples of available connectors: Slack (search messages, read channels), Microsoft 365 (access files, search emails), HubSpot (search CRM data), and others depending on your organization's setup.

‚Ä¢ Information is in connected systems

‚Ä¢ You need current/live data

‚Ä¢ You're searching but not sure where it is

‚Ä¢ You want Claude to search multiple sources

‚úó Upload Manually When:

‚Ä¢ File is on your computer

‚Ä¢ You have a specific document to analyze

‚Ä¢ Document isn't in connected system

‚Ä¢ You want to ensure specific version used

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Privacy Considerations

When you enable connectors, Claude can access anything you have permission to see in those systems. For example:

  • Any chat channels you're a member of (including direct messages)
  • Any documents or pages you can read in your knowledge systems
  • Any CRM data you have access to
  • Any project management issues you can see
  • Any files and emails in your productivity systems

Claude respects your existing permissions but CAN access this data once enabled. Review what's in your systems before enabling connectors.

Working with Complex Documents

Three Key Techniques

1. Comparing Documents: Upload two versions and ask what changed, what was added, what was removed.

2. Synthesizing Multiple Sources: Pull information from different systems (Confluence doc + Slack messages + support tickets) for integrated analysis.

3. Building Document Libraries: Create a Project with core docs, then use connectors to pull additional context as needed.

Citation and Source Tracking

When working with multiple documents, ask Claude to cite sources:

"Analyze these 5 customer feedback documents. For each insight you provide, tell me which document it came from."

Why this matters: You can verify claims and go back to the source if needed.

‚ö†Ô∏è Verification Requirement

When Claude synthesizes information from multiple sources, verify key claims before sharing externally. Multi-source synthesis can occasionally blend or misattribute information. Always spot-check critical facts.

üí° Strategic Use of Multi-Document Analysis

  • Use for high-value analysis: Quarterly reviews, strategic planning, complex research
  • Use simpler approaches for: Simple information lookup (just search one source)
  • Match the technique complexity to the task value
Combining Tools for Powerful Workflows

Projects + Skills + Connectors

Each tool has a distinct role:

  • Projects: "What's the background and context?"
  • Skills: "How should I do this task?"
  • Custom Instructions: "How should I communicate?"
  • Artifacts: "How should I present the output?"
  • Connectors: "What current information do I need from our systems?"

Example Workflow

Setup:

  • Project: "Q4 Business Review" with financial data and metrics
  • Skill: Brand Guidelines for formatting
  • Connectors: Enabled for your knowledge management and team chat systems
  • Custom Instructions: Your communication preferences

Prompt in Project: "Create a Q4 business review presentation for executives. Pull the latest product roadmap from our knowledge system. Use our brand guidelines."

What happens:

  • Claude loads Q4 data from Project
  • Claude pulls current roadmap via connector
  • Claude activates Brand Guidelines Skill
  • Claude applies your Custom Instructions
  • Claude creates branded Artifact with current data

Result: Contextually aware, properly formatted, brand-compliant presentation with current data

Building Reusable Prompt Templates

What Are Prompt Templates?

Fill-in-the-blank prompts for tasks you do repeatedly. Create once, reuse many times.

Example Template Structure:

Write a response to this customer email about [ISSUE].

Context:
- Customer tier: [Enterprise/Pro/Standard]
- Issue severity: [Critical/High/Medium/Low]
- Our fault: [Yes/No]

Tone: [Empathetic/Professional/Technical]

Include: acknowledgment, explanation, fix plan, timeline, next steps

üí° Pro Tip: Turn Templates into Skills

  • Save your best templates as Skills
  • Claude will use them automatically when relevant
  • No need to paste the template each time
  • Team members can use the same Skill for consistency
